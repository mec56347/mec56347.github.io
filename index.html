The Atlanta Falcons

The Atlanta Falcons are members of the National Football Conference (NFC) South Division.They joined the NFL on June 30, 1965 as an expansion team, and are considered one of the oldest NFL franchises in The Deep South along with The Miami Dolphins. The Falcons have a total record of 316-414-6, and have only ever been to one Super Bowl, in 1998, but has yet to actually win one.

The Falcons play their home games at the Georgia Dome, in downtown Atlanta Georgia, but currently there is a new stadium being built called the Mercedes-Benz Stadium which the Falcons are set to start playing at, in the beginning of the 2017-2018 season. While the Falcons play their home games at the Georgia Dome, their headquarters for their Franchise is based out of Flowery Branch, Georgia, on the outskirts of rural Atlanta.

The Falcons, in the current season, are 6-3 and are widely regarded as having the most highpowered offense in the league. Team leaders, Matt Ryan, Julio Jones, Devonta Freeman, and Vic Beasley, just to name a few, are the face of the current franchise and are main reasons why the current team is having as much success as it is. Julio and Matt Ryan have both been considered as MVP candidates and will, hopefully, lead this team to the franchise's first ever Super Bowl Win!
